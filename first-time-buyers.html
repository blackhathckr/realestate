First-Time Buyer FAQs

Answers to common questions from first-time home buyers

Typically, banks finance up to 80-90% of the property value, so you'll need to arrange 10-20% as down payment. For example, on a ₹50 lakh property, you would need ₹5-10 lakh as down payment. Some government schemes may offer higher financing for first-time buyers.

Beyond the property price, budget for:

  • Stamp duty and registration (5-7% of property value)
  • Legal and documentation fees (₹20,000-₹50,000)
  • Interior work and furnishings (₹5-15 lakh depending on size)
  • Maintenance deposit (varies by society)
  • Moving costs and utility connections

Ready-to-move properties offer immediate occupancy but may be more expensive. Under-construction properties are typically 10-20% cheaper but carry completion risk. Consider:

  • Your timeline for moving in
  • Builder's track record for under-construction projects
  • Payment plan flexibility
  • Price difference and appreciation potential

Essential documents to verify include:

  • Title deed and chain of ownership
  • Approved building plans and completion certificate
  • Encumbrance certificate (last 30 years)
  • Property tax receipts
  • Society NOC (for apartments)
  • RERA registration for new projects

We recommend engaging a lawyer to verify all documents.

To increase your loan eligibility:

  • Maintain a credit score above 750
  • Reduce existing debts (credit cards, other loans)
  • Include all income sources (bonuses, rentals, etc.)
  • Add a co-applicant with good income
  • Choose a longer tenure (reduces EMI burden)
  • Show stable employment history (2+ years preferred)
